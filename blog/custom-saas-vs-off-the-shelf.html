Custom SaaS vs off-the-shelf: when to build your own

Vyzion Systems · Digital growth team

Scaling businesses outgrow generic tools. Here's when custom software pays off.

Off-the-shelf tools work at the start, but growing businesses often hit a ceiling: limited integrations, rigid workflows and costs that scale with users.

When generic SaaS stops scaling

Custom software makes sense when your processes are unique, when you need specific integrations or when the ready-made tool costs more than building.

Signals you need custom software

  • Spreadsheet workflows and manual handoffs eating team hours
  • Billing per seat outpaces value as you grow
  • Missing integrations with your CRM, payments or ops stack
If you're duct-taping five tools together, you may already be paying more than building the right platform once.
